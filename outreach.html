Teaching & Outreach

A selection of teaching, mentoring, and science communication projects focused on water systems, data literacy, and applied environmental engineering.

Teaching Experience

During my PhD, I served as a teaching assistant for Civil and Environmental Engineering Decision Making. Course materials are available in the course syllabus. I also filled in for lectures at UW-Madison on linear optimization and hydroclimatology.

K-12 Lesson: Machine Learning and Remote Sensing

This classroom activity is designed for 2-3 class periods. Students create samples with food coloring or collect grab samples, then use phone images in Google Colab to train simple models relating reflectance to chlorophyll concentration. Lesson materials and notebook are available in the project repository.

Presented at the Wisconsin Society of Science Teachers Conference (La Crosse, WI, 2024)

Public Outreach: Hydrology Demonstrations

I collaborated with Madison-area schools during the pandemic using a flood tank demonstration through Saturday Science and the UW-Madison Engineering Expo program.
