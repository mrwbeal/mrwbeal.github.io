Research Program

My research integrates hydroclimatology, remote sensing, and machine learning to develop early-warning systems and decision-support tools for aquatic ecosystems and public health. I focus on understanding how climate variability propagates through water systems to shape biological outcomes across scales.

1. Forecasting & Early Warning Systems for Aquatic Ecosystems

I develop forecasting systems that extend decision-making horizons for water managers from days to seasons. This work centers on harmful algal blooms, where earlier warning allows agencies to allocate resources, plan monitoring, and mitigate public health risk. I also develop spatially distributed harmful algae forecasts at satellite (300 m) resolution.

Key Contributions
  • Sub-seasonal cyanobacteria forecasting for Lake Mendota (Harmful Algae, 2021)
  • Seasonal forecasting framework (Lake & Reservoir Management, 2022)
  • Regional extension to 178 lakes (Water Research, 2023)
  • Satellite pixel-scale forecasts for Florida lakes (Harmful Algae, 2025)

2. Remote Sensing of Water Quality

I combine satellite imagery with in-situ monitoring to retrieve algal pigments and dissolved oxygen, enabling scalable lake-wide water quality assessment. This work integrates Sentinel-2 and Sentinel-3 observations with machine learning models.

Key Contribution
  • Machine-learning retrievals of pigments and dissolved oxygen (Water Resources Research, 2023)

3. Climate-Ecosystem-Health Dynamics

I extend hydroclimate forecasting to public-health applications, focusing on mosquito-borne disease risk including West Nile virus in the U.S. and dengue fever in Colombia. This work shows climate-based machine-learning models can outperform traditional approaches at seasonal timescales.

Key Projects
  • CDC West Nile Virus Forecasting Challenge (2023)
  • Dengue forecasting with UW-Madison Global Health Institute and One Health Center Colombia (GeoHealth, 2025)
