Research Program

My research focuses on forecasting and remote sensing of water quality. I build early-warning systems for harmful algal blooms and integrate satellite observations with in-situ monitoring to support operational decisions.

Forecasting

Sub-seasonal to seasonal harmful algal bloom early warning systems.

Remote Sensing

Satellite + in-situ retrievals for pigments and oxygen at lake scale.

Operations

Decision-ready products built for agencies and applied monitoring programs.

1. Forecasting & Early Warning Systems

Harmful algal bloom forecasts that extend management lead time from days to seasons.

+

I develop forecasting systems that extend decision-making horizons for water managers from days to seasons. This work focuses on harmful algal blooms, where earlier warning supports planning, monitoring design, and risk communication. I also build spatially distributed forecast products at satellite-scale resolution.

Key Contributions
  • Sub-seasonal cyanobacteria forecasting for Lake Mendota (Harmful Algae, 2021)
  • Seasonal forecasting framework (Lake & Reservoir Management, 2022)
  • Regional extension to 178 lakes (Water Research, 2023)
  • Satellite pixel-scale forecasts for Florida lakes (Harmful Algae, 2025)

2. Remote Sensing of Water Quality

Satellite-enabled retrievals of water quality indicators linked to field observations.

+

I combine satellite imagery with in-situ monitoring to retrieve algal pigments and dissolved oxygen, enabling scalable lake-wide water quality assessment. This work integrates Sentinel-2 and Sentinel-3 observations with machine learning models for robust, transferable workflows.

Key Contribution
  • Machine-learning retrievals of pigments and dissolved oxygen (Water Resources Research, 2023)

3. Translating Models into Operations

Decision-ready forecast and remote sensing products for applied lake management.

+

A core goal of my research is operational impact. I build workflows that connect model outputs and Earth-observation products to agency decision points, including sampling prioritization, risk communication, and ongoing performance evaluation.

Operational Priorities
  • Extend forecast horizons while preserving practical skill
  • Deliver lake- and pixel-scale products for targeted monitoring
  • Combine reproducible modeling workflows with routine observation streams
